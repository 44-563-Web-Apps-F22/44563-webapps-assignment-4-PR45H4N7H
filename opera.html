The Mikado

ACT II — Ko-Ko's Garden

Enter Pitti-Sing and Peep-Bo.

YUM

YUM. Yes, everything seems to smile upon me. I am to be married to-day to the man I love best and I believe I am the very happiest girl in Japan!

PEEP

PEEP. The happiest girl indeed, for she is indeed to be envied who has attained happiness in all but perfection.

YUM

YUM. In "all but" perfection?

PEEP

PEEP. Well, dear, it can't be denied that the fact that your husband is to be beheaded in a month is, in its way, a drawback. It does seem to take the top off it, you know.

PITTI

PITTI. I don't know about that. It all depends!

PEEP

PEEP. At all events, he will find it a drawback.

PITTI

PITTI. Not necessarily. Bless you, it all depends!

YUM

YUM.

(in tears).

I think it very indelicate of you to refer to such a subject on such a day. If my married happiness is to be—to be—

PEEP

PEEP. Cut short.

YUM

YUM. Well, cut short—in a month, can't you let me forget it?

(Weeping.)

MADRIGAL

YUM-YUM, PITTI-SING, NANKI-POO, and PISH-TUSH Brightly dawns our wedding day; Joyous hour, we give thee greeting! Whither, whither art thou fleeting? Fickle moment, prithee stay! What though mortal joys be hollow? Pleasures come, if sorrows follow: Though the tocsin sound, ere long, Ding dong! Ding dong! Yet until the shadows fall Over one and over all, Sing a merry madrigal— A madrigal! Fal-la—fal-la! etc.

(Ending in tears.)
